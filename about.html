My story

Why I became a psychologist

I've always been drawn to the inner world — the way we think, feel, and make sense of our lives. Growing up, I was a deeply sensitive child navigating environments that often seemed to demand more than what I felt I had within me. For a long time, that left me feeling misunderstood, insignificant, and disconnected — and without realizing it, I often pushed away the very people who cared for me.

Those experiences became the beginning of a deeper journey inward. Through reading, reflection, and doing my own inner work, I learned how powerful self-awareness, healing, and supportive relationships can be in shaping the way we experience ourselves and others.

Today, I've built a life rooted in confidence, emotional safety, and genuine connection — one where differences are met with understanding rather than judgment. That journey is a big part of what led me to psychology, and why I care so deeply about creating spaces where others can feel seen, supported, and empowered in their own growth.

The name itself reflects my philosophy: we are all, in our own way, stories in the process of blooming — growing through the seasons, sometimes slowly, sometimes in unexpected directions, but always reaching toward light.

My approach to therapy

I work from a place of genuine curiosity and deep respect. My role isn't to have all the answers — it's to create a space where you can explore, reflect, and find your own. I believe that insight without action changes little, and action without insight rarely lasts. Together, we work on both.

I draw on a range of evidence-based frameworks — including Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR), and Transactional Analysis (TA) — but always adapt these to fit the individual in front of me, not the other way around.

Who I work with

I work with adults, adolescents, and children navigating a wide range of experiences — anxiety, depression, stress, relationship difficulties, life transitions, parenting challenges, workplace burnout, identity questions, and more.

I also work with teachers, HR professionals, and corporate teams through structured workshops and coaching programs.

A personal note

Seeking help is one of the most courageous things a person can do. If you're here, reading this, you've already taken a step. I hope this practice feels like a place where you can truly exhale — be yourself, ask the hard questions, and begin to bloom.
